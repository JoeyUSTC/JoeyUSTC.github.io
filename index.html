I am presently appointed as a postdoctoral research fellow at the AMPC center of KAUST, conducting extensive research on electrocatalytic conversion of CH4 and CO2

Simultaneously, I am engaged in acquiring proficiency in utilizing advanced simulation tools, such as COMSOL, to perform numerical analysis on electrocatalytic devices, including fuel cells, electrolysis cells, and gas diffusion electrodes. Through a combination of artificial intelligence and simulation techniques, I aspire to develop highly efficient electrocatalytic devices in the forthcoming future.

Before assuming my current position, I earned my doctoral and bachelor's degrees from USTC, where my research was concentrated on CO2 electrocatalytic reduction, water electrolysis for hydrogen generation, and hydrogen fuel cells during my doctoral tenure.
